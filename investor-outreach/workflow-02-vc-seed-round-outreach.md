## Phase 3: Partner Meetings (Months 3-5)

### 3.1 First Partner Meeting (30-45 min)

**Meeting Objectives**
- Validate VC is the right fit
- Get feedback on pitch and business
- Understand VC's process and timeline
- Secure follow-up meeting or partner intro

**Meeting Structure**
1. **Intro** (3 min): Background, why you started DWS IQ 6
2. **Pitch** (10 min): Problem, solution, traction, ask (abbreviated deck)
3. **Deep dive** (15 min): Whatever partner wants to explore (market, tech, team)
4. **Q&A** (10 min): Answer questions, address concerns
5. **Next steps** (2 min): Ask about process, timeline, what they need to move forward

**Key Questions to Ask the VC**
- What's your investment process and typical timeline?
- What would you need to see to move forward?
- Who else at the firm should I speak with?
- What are your reservations or concerns?
- Do you lead rounds or follow? (Critical question!)
- What's your typical check size for a company at our stage?
- How do you support portfolio companies post-investment?

**After the Meeting**
- [ ] Send thank you email within 2 hours
- [ ] Attach any requested materials (deck, model, case studies)
- [ ] Propose next steps (partner meeting, due diligence, site visit)
- [ ] Update CRM with detailed notes
- [ ] Set follow-up reminder (3-5 days if they don't respond)

### 3.2 Partner Meeting & Pitch (45-60 min)

**Who Attends**
- [ ] You (CEO)
- [ ] Co-founder or CTO (if applicable)
- [ ] VC: Multiple partners (2-4 people typically)

**Meeting Structure**
1. **Intro** (5 min): Quick round of intros
2. **Full pitch** (10 min): Complete deck walkthrough
3. **Product demo** (10 min): Show DWS IQ 6 in action (live or video)
4. **Q&A** (20 min): Technical, market, competitive, team questions
5. **Next steps** (5 min): Discuss due diligence, timeline, decision process

**Common Partner Questions**

**Market Questions**
- "How big is the TAM? How did you calculate it?"
- "What happens if the EU delays the 2028 mandate?"
- "Why would customers pay for this vs. free tools?"

**Technical Questions**
- "Why edge vs. cloud? What if cloud gets cheaper?"
- "How defensible is your tech? Can Google build this?"
- "What's your infrastructure cost at scale?"

**Competitive Questions**
- "Who else is solving this? Why will you win?"
- "What about [Competitor X]?"
- "What's your moat beyond tech?"

**Traction Questions**
- "What's your customer acquisition cost?"
- "How long is your sales cycle?"
- "What's your churn? LTV/CAC ratio?"

**Team Questions**
- "Why are you the right team to build this?"

### 3.4 Navigate the Decision Process

**Typical VC Process Timeline**
- Week 1: First partner meeting
- Week 2: Partner meeting + initial diligence
- Week 3-4: Deep due diligence
- Week 5: Investment committee (IC) presentation
- Week 6: Term sheet (if approved)

**Red Flags (Pull Back If You See These)**
- VC is taking >6 weeks with no clear timeline
- VC keeps asking for more information without moving forward
- VC won't commit to leading or following
- VC wants to "stay in touch" (usually means no)

**How to Create Urgency**
- Share other VC interest (carefully, don't bluff)
- Set a deadline for closing the round
- Show momentum (customer wins, new pilots, revenue growth)
- External events (Ecosummit pitch, Slush deadline, pilot program start date)

## Phase 4: Term Sheet & Negotiation (Months 5-6)

### 4.1 Understand VC Term Sheets

**Key Terms to Negotiate**

**Valuation**
- **Pre-money valuation**: â‚¬5M-â‚¬7M (based on traction)
- **Post-money valuation**: Pre-money + investment amount
- **Dilution**: Aim for <20-25% dilution in Seed round

**Investment Structure**
- **Priced round**: Preferred stock with specific terms
- **SAFE/Convertible**: Less common for VC Seed, but possible

**Investor Rights**
- **Board seat**: Expect lead investor to request 1 board seat
- **Pro rata rights**: Investor can invest in future rounds
- **Information rights**: Regular financial reporting (standard)
- **Liquidation preference**: 1Ã— non-participating (standard), avoid >1Ã— or participating preferred

**Founder Protection**
- **Vesting**: 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff (standard)
- **Anti-dilution**: Weighted average (better for founders) vs. full ratchet (avoid)
- **Drag-along rights**: Majority can force minority to sell (standard)
- **Employee option pool**: 10-15% post-money (negotiate to be post-money, not pre)

**What to Accept**
- 1Ã— non-participating liquidation preference
- Board seat for lead investor
- Pro rata rights for all investors
- Standard vesting (4-year, 1-year cliff)
- Weighted average anti-dilution

**What to Avoid**
- >1Ã— liquidation preference
- Participating preferred (investor gets preference + pro rata share)
- Full ratchet anti-dilution
- >2 board seats for investors at Seed stage
- Blocking rights on routine decisions

### 4.2 Review with Lawyer

**Find a Startup Lawyer**
- [ ] Get referrals from other founders or VCs
- [ ] Look for someone experienced in VC deals
- [ ] Budget â‚¬3K-â‚¬10K for legal fees (negotiable based on complexity)

**Lawyer Review Checklist**
- [ ] Have lawyer review term sheet before signing
- [ ] Negotiate any problematic terms
- [ ] Understand all clauses and implications
- [ ] Get legal opinion in writing

### 4.3 Negotiate Terms

**Negotiation Tips**
- Don't negotiate on every point (pick your battles)
- Valuation and dilution are most important
- Be respectful but firm on founder protections
- Understand that some terms are "market standard" (don't fight those)
- Get multiple term sheets if possible (creates leverage)

### 4.4 Close the Round

**Lead vs. Follow-On Investors**
- **Lead investor**: Sets terms, does diligence, commits first (â‚¬200K-â‚¬500K)
- **Follow-on investors**: Follow lead's terms, smaller checks (â‚¬50K-â‚¬200K)

**Closing Process**
1. **Term sheet signed**: Non-binding agreement on key terms
2. **Legal docs drafted**: VC's lawyers draft full investment agreement
3. **Legal review**: Your lawyers review and negotiate
4. **Final docs signed**: Shareholders agreement, stock purchase agreement, etc.
5. **Wire transfer**: Funds transferred to company account
6. **Close announcement**: Announce on LinkedIn, press release (optional)

**Timeline**: 2-4 weeks from term sheet to close (if no major issues)
