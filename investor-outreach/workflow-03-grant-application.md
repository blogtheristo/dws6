## Phase 5: Evaluation & Award (Months 6-18)

### 5.1 During Evaluation Period

**What Happens**
- Expert reviewers evaluate proposal (2-4 months)
- Possible interview or pitch presentation (EIC, Innovation Fund)
- Site visits or due diligence (rare but possible)
- Final decision by grant committee

**Your Actions**
- [ ] Monitor email and grant portal daily
- [ ] Prepare for possible interview (practice pitch)
- [ ] Keep business metrics updated (in case asked)
- [ ] Don't count on grant money (keep fundraising)

### 5.2 If Invited to Interview (EIC Accelerator)

**Interview Preparation**
- [ ] Review proposal in detail (refresh your memory)
- [ ] Prepare 10-minute pitch
- [ ] Anticipate evaluator questions:
  - Technical feasibility
  - Market validation
  - Team capability
  - Financial projections
  - Risk mitigation
- [ ] Practice with advisors (mock interview)
- [ ] Prepare demo (if allowed)

**Interview Day**
- [ ] Dress professionally (video or in-person)
- [ ] Bring backup materials (laptop, printed proposal)
- [ ] Answer questions confidently and concisely
- [ ] Show passion and mission-driven purpose
- [ ] Thank evaluators and ask about timeline

### 5.3 Grant Award

**If Awarded**
- [ ] Celebrate! (Announce on LinkedIn, press release)
- [ ] Review grant agreement carefully (get legal review)
- [ ] Negotiate any problematic terms (rare but possible)
- [ ] Sign agreement and submit banking details
- [ ] Set up grant accounting (separate tracking for grant funds)
- [ ] Assign project manager for grant reporting
- [ ] Schedule kick-off meeting with grant officer
- [ ] Thank consortium partners and collaborators

**Grant Agreement Terms** (Typical)
- Co-funding requirement (40-60% from EU, rest from you)
- Eligible costs (salaries, equipment, travel, subcontracting)
- Reporting requirements (quarterly or annual)
- Deliverables and milestones
- IP rights (usually background IP yours, foreground IP shared)
- Audit rights (EU can audit your use of funds)

**Set Up Grant Management**
- [ ] Create separate budget tracking for grant
- [ ] Hire accountant familiar with EU grants (if large grant)
- [ ] Document all expenses (receipts, timesheets)
- [ ] Use time tracking for personnel costs
- [ ] Set reminders for reporting deadlines
- [ ] Assign someone to liaise with grant officer

### 5.4 If Rejected

**Learn from Rejection**
- [ ] Request evaluator feedback (usually provided)
- [ ] Identify weaknesses in application
- [ ] Improve for next application
- [ ] Consider resubmitting in next call (if allowed)
- [ ] Don't take it personally (low success rates are normal)

**Next Steps**
- [ ] Apply to other grants (keep pipeline full)
- [ ] Focus on revenue and customer traction
- [ ] Use evaluation feedback to improve pitch
- [ ] Network with successful grant recipients for tips
